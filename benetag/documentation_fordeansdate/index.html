Consider the tags that you find on pieces of clothing. They probably say something like “Made in Country X” and little else. You might see a barcode that is generic to all units of the same product. But there is so much more to know about a product than basic facts that are the same for all units. Who made it? Under what working conditions? How far did the product travel to get to you? BeneTags will replace these mostly ignored tags with simple-to-scan QR codes or RFID tags. With the click of a button, the consumer is brought to a page that tells the story of how the product was made, opening up a whole new world of information.

BeneTag empowers consumers and producers. Producers have the opportunity to gain viral publicity and to maintain relationships with consumers beyond the checkout counter. Consumers are now able to base their purchasing decisions on considerations they care about, and they can use their purchasing power to demand that producers commit to ethical practices. The possibilities that such a platform affords are endless.
